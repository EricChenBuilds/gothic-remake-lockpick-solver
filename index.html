      How to Solve a Gothic 1 Remake Lock

      1. Pick the PLATES count (4 / 5 / 6 / 7) that matches the chest in front of you.
      2. On Plate 1, click the hole where the start pin currently sits. The target pin defaults to Hole 4.
      3. For each plate, set the Link Grid cells to None, Same, or Opposite based on what the linked plate does in-game. Press SOLVE LOCK and read the result in the right-hand panel.
      4. Tips for reading the link grid in-game

      Reading the link grid is the part most players get wrong on their first chest. Same means the linked plate moves in the same direction as the plate you press, so if Plate 1 moves right and the link from Plate 1 to Plate 2 is Same, Plate 2 also moves right. Opposite means the linked plate moves in the opposite direction, so Plate 2 would move left while Plate 1 moves right. None means the plates are not linked and pressing one does not move the other at all. On this page the link grid uses three states: None (empty cell), Same (S in green), and Opposite (O in red). Click any non-diagonal cell to cycle through them until it matches what you see in-game. If the solution you get here does not match the lock state in-game after pressing the listed moves, the most common cause is a single Same/Opposite mistake somewhere in the grid, with a wrong start pin on Plate 1 as the second most common cause.
